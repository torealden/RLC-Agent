## Key Frameworks Extracted

### 1. BBD Capacity Expansion / Biodiesel Rationalization
RD capacity doubled YoY to 1.95B gal (Jun 2022), predicted 2.7B by YE 2022. Biodiesel stable through 2023 (supplemental mandate + blend wall support), rapid decline after. Integrated producers + state mandates set floor.

### 2. Ethanol Blend Wall -> BBD Demand Linkage
890M gal ethanol shortfall (2022) forces additional 555M gal BBD. Growing to 935M/580M in 2023. Structural demand support for biodiesel margins.

### 3. RD Import Assumption (Dominant Forecast Variable)
Single most impactful assumption: higher RD imports reduced domestic feedstock demand across ALL commodities by billions of lbs. SBO demand cut 4-8.8B lbs, tallow up to 1.1B lbs. Maximum uncertainty post-2024 BTC expiration.

### 4. SBO Structural Tightness
US SBO stocks-to-use forecast avg 5.6% vs 8.2% prior 10yr. Soybean acreage must avg 94.9M acres (historically unprecedented). Any below-trendline yield creates immediate deficit. Crush capacity must expand to 2.6B bu by 2027.

### 5. LCFS Credit Bank / CARB Intervention
Credit bank at 10.35M tonnes (Q1 2022), growing to 12-14M by YE 2023, possibly 20M by 2024. CARB intervention virtually certain. Proposals: 25-30% CI target, jet fuel inclusion, crop-based feedstock cap.

### 6. UCO/YG Production Data Gap
No reliable official UCO/YG production data. Residual calculation makes non-biofuel demand go negative. Historical production cut by 1.1B lbs. Model flagged for significant revision.

### 7. CI Score / Feedstock Mix Divergence
CA feedstock mix (80-90% fats/greases) dramatically different from national (31-33%). If IRA shifts national toward CA mix, needs +5.5B lbs additional fats/greases -- physically impossible without SBO.

### 8. Non-Biofuel Demand Compression
Fat/grease non-biofuel use cut avg 1.3B lbs/yr as RD absorbs supply. Traditional industrial uses being displaced. Ceiling: non-biofuel industries will eventually outbid biofuel -> RINs must compensate.
