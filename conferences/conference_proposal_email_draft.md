## Email Body:

Dear [Conference Organizer / Program Committee],

I'd like to propose a presentation for [Conference Name]: **"AI Isn't Coming for Your Job. I Am." — A Commodity Analyst's Experience Building an AI-Powered Research Platform.**

**The premise:** I'm a commodity analyst, not a developer. Over the past year, I've built a production-grade market intelligence system — 187,000 lines of code, 4 million data points, 22 commodities tracked across 35 years of history — without writing a single line of code by hand. Everything was built through natural language conversation with an AI.

**What the audience gets:**
- A live look at the system: automated data collection from 30+ sources (USDA, EIA, EPA, CFTC, Census, CONAB), a knowledge graph encoding analyst intuition, and AI-generated reports and graphics
- An honest assessment of what AI can and cannot do for commodity analysts
- A practical framework: what to automate first, how to encode your expertise, and why domain knowledge is the moat — not the technology
- The forecast comparison framework: tracking human vs. AI vs. USDA accuracy over time

**Why this matters now:** The tools are available to everyone. The question is whether commodity professionals will use them before their competitors do. This talk is designed to inspire action, not fear.

**Format:** I'm flexible — this works as a 20-minute focused talk or a 45-minute deep dive with audience Q&A. Happy to tailor to your audience.

**About me:** I'm Tore Alden, founder of Round Lakes Companies. I've spent 25+ years in agricultural commodity markets covering oilseeds, biofuels, and feedstock economics. I've presented at Global Grains, Fastmarkets' Biofuels Americas, Biofuels Europe, and Global Grains Conferences, Livestock Marketing Information Center's Industry Outlook, Informa's Annual Client Conference, American Fats and Oils Association's Annual Meeting. You can find videos of weekly webinars I did at Fastmarkets by searching for Fastmarkets' "Fats, Fuels & Oils" webinar series on YouTube.

I'd welcome the chance to discuss this further. A brief deck overview is available upon request.
