## The one line to hold onto
**The $10k is not the prize.** One client nets modest cash. The assets are: (1) the **channel** —
Pepsi is CPG client #1 of N, Helios reaches food buyers you don't; (2) the **credential** — a
Fortune-50 reference; (3) the **track record** — every forecast feeds the forecast book. Spend
leverage on those, not on squeezing the Pepsi fee.

## Frame (anchor in the first few minutes)
"In May we figured out we're adjacent, not overlapping. Pepsi is the first place that adjacency
turns into something real — you've got the food-CPG relationships, I've got the oils-and-feedstock
forecasting engine. I want to set this up so it works for both of us **and scales past Pepsi**."

This reframes the meeting from "vendor terms for one account" to "how do we build a channel."

## Objectives, in priority order
1. **Listen first.** What did Helios promise Pepsi (deliverable, cadence, format, who reads it,
   what "good" looks like)? What is Pepsi actually *deciding* — coverage timing, budget, hedge ratios?
2. **Establish the channel.** Get them to confirm Pepsi is the first of more food clients wanting
   the same oils coverage. This is the most important answer in the room.
3. **Fair, scalable economics.** Price for reuse (build once, sell many), not bespoke labor.
4. **Protect the core.** License the *output*, not the *method*. Reference rights. Carve-out so this
   never restricts your own veg-oil work (Feedstock Report / HB / consulting).
5. **Define accountability + deliverable shape.** Probabilistic guidance + tracked record (not point
   bets). Push for *you on a monthly call* — voice = value + anti-disintermediation.

## Positioning / talking points
- **Differentiated lens (lead with this):** veg-oil prices are now set by *biofuel demand* — SBO/canola
  pulled by renewable-diesel feedstock competition, palm by Indonesian B40→B50 + BBD substitution.
  Pepsi's other sources (Argus/OPIS/banks) give commodity price forecasts; you give "your frying-oil
  cost is a function of RD policy and feedstock competition, 3 months out." This is the premium frame —
  do not let it become generic price forecasting.
- **Build-once-sell-many:** "I build the oils forecast once; it serves Pepsi and every CPG client you
  add at near-zero marginal cost. Let's structure economics so adding clients improves *both* our take."
- **You as the analytical voice:** a human on the monthly call who explains the call is far stickier
  and harder to replace than a data feed — and it's where the credential lives.

## Economics — the crux (where these go sideways)
- **Don't name a number first.** Get their proposal; know your floor; don't anchor low for the logo.
- **Principles to state:** you are the product (the entire analytical output); Helios brings the client
  relationship + packaging + distribution. Reasonable content-licensing splits land 30–50%+ to the
  content provider — but argue *structure*, not a single %: **per-client floor + economics that scale
  with the client count.** Flat-fee-per-account caps you while they scale the book on your engine.
- **Reframe up:** "The Pepsi fee matters less to me than the channel terms. Get the structure right and
  I'll be reasonable on Pepsi itself."

## Anticipated Helios questions → recommended framing
- **"What would you charge / what's your cut?"** → "Depends on what you've promised Pepsi and whether
  this scales to more clients — tell me that and I'll give you a structure, not just a number. I'd rather
  price the channel than the account."
- **"Can you walk us through how you derive the forecasts?"** → "You get the call and the narrative —
  the drivers, the ranges, the risks. The engine underneath is the part that took a year to build; that
  stays mine. You're licensing the output, which is what Pepsi actually needs."
- **"What if the forecast is wrong?"** → "They will be, individually — anyone who says otherwise is
  selling. The product is probabilistic guidance with a *measured* track record over time; I run a
  forecast book that reconciles every call against realized data. Judge it on the record, not a month."
- **"Does this conflict with your other work?"** → "No — same engine powers my own research. I need a
  carve-out that I keep using it for my products. That's not yours to fence, and it's why I'm cheap to
  run for you."
- **"Could you cover [more oils / more commodities]?"** → "Yes — SBO/palm/canola is the core; the rest
  of those complexes I already model. More scope is exactly the channel conversation."
- **"Can we white-label / not name you to Pepsi?"** → "Fine for now, but I keep reference rights — at
  minimum 'powers forecasts for a Fortune-50 food manufacturer.' Invisibility I won't do."
